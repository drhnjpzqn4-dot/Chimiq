
1. 🔥 **Barrier destruction** — Mixing retinol + AHA/BHA at the same time destroys your skin barrier. Leads to redness, peeling, and long-term sensitivity. Extremely common mistake.

2. 🧪 **Retinol burn** — Retinol is powerful. Without a buffer or gradual intro, it causes chemical burns, especially around the eyes and mouth. Often mistaken for "purging."

3. ☀️ **Hidden sun sensitivity** — AHAs, retinol, and vitamin C all make skin photosensitive. Without SPF, these ingredients actively damage skin in daylight — the opposite of what you paid for.

4. 🧬 **Endocrine disruptors** — Parabens, benzophenone, and phthalates mimic estrogen. Found in hundreds of mainstream products. Long-term hormonal disruption, especially concerning for teens and young women.

5. ☣️ **Formaldehyde releasers** — DMDM Hydantoin, Quaternium-15, and Imidazolidinyl Urea slowly release formaldehyde — a known carcinogen — into your skin. Legal in most countries. Common in cheap moisturizers and shampoos.

6. 💊 **Transdermal absorption** — Your skin is not a barrier to chemicals — it's a delivery system. Medicine patches (nicotine, hormones, painkillers) work because skin absorbs substances directly into the bloodstream. Your serum does too.

7. 🌸 **Fragrance — the hidden culprit** — "Fragrance" or "Parfum" on a label can hide up to 3,000 undisclosed chemicals. It's the #1 cause of contact dermatitis and the most common allergen in cosmetics.

8. ⚡ **Over-exfoliation spiral** — AHA + BHA + physical scrub + retinol in the same routine = stripped, reactive skin. The more irritated skin gets, the more product people buy to fix it. The spiral.

9. 🧫 **Nanoparticles** — Nano-sized zinc oxide and titanium dioxide in sunscreens penetrate beyond the skin surface. Research on long-term effects is still ongoing. You deserve to know when they're in your product.

10. 💧 **Mixing vitamin C wrong** — L-ascorbic acid (vitamin C) is unstable and degrades fast when mixed with certain ingredients. Most vitamin C products oxidize before they do anything. You're often paying for expensive orange water.
