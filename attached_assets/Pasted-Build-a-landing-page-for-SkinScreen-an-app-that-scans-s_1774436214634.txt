Build a landing page for SkinScreen — an app that scans skincare ingredient lists and warns users about dangerous ingredient combinations.

THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE:
The average woman (and even teenagers!) uses 4-7 skincare products daily = up to 400 ingredients on their skin. No consumer app today checks how these products interact with each other. Influencers push more products = more spending = more skin problems. SkinScreen breaks that spiral.

OUR ANGLE: Don ’t mix chemicals you don’t know how they react together. And do you really know what ingredients you put on your sensitive skin? 

WHAT TO BUILD:
A single-page landing site with these sections:

1. HERO: Bold headline + subheadline + waitlist email signup form
   - Headline: "40 products. 400 ingredients. Do you know what they do to each other?"
   - Sub: "SkinScreen scans your skincare and finds dangerous combinations — before they find your skin."
   - CTA button: "Join the waitlist"

2. THE SPIRAL: Visual showing "The Skincare Spiral" — how one product leads to 5 more
   - Step 1: Influencer recommends serum
   - Step 2: Skin dries out → buy moisturizer  
   - Step 3: Moisturizer doesn't penetrate → buy booster
   - Step 4: Booster + serum = breakout → buy calming cream
   - Step 5: 40 jars on the shelf, skin worse than before
   - SkinScreen breaks the cycle

3. HOW IT WORKS: 3 steps with icons
   - Scan your ingredient list
   - Add your other products  
   - See what combinations are safe — and what to avoid

4. THE NUMBERS: Stats section
   - "$500 billion beauty industry built on confusion"
   - "Average teen spends $300/year on products that may conflict"
   - "SkinScreen users use 40% fewer products"

5. WAITLIST: "Be first to know when SkinScreen launches"
   - Email input + signup button
   - "Join 200+ people waiting for smarter skincare" (social proof)

DESIGN:
- Clean, minimal, Apple-inspired — lots of white space
- Accent color: soft sage green (#7BAF7A) — natural, trustworthy, not clinical
- Typography: serif for headlines (elegant), sans-serif for body (clean)
- NO dark mode
- Mobile-first, looks perfect on iPhone
- Small animated elements — the "spiral" visualization should have a subtle animation showing products piling up

TONE: Smart friend, not scary. Empowering, not shaming. Science-backed, not preachy.

Make it feel premium. This should look like a funded startup, not a school project.
